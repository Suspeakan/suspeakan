Пулйлоцкацы ҳарлэтҳой                                        Adverbs

In СЫПЕҖ, adverbs are, as one would expect, a class (сорат) of (ду) words (йлоцкацы), which modify verbs (пулйлоцкацы) by encoding manner or time, among other things. Adverbs modify independantly, that means if two adverbs are stringed together in a “typical” adverbial phrase they don’t modify eachother or add together, they are independant. For example, “тамой ли ѳинецицмызат синилё мелодиё”, meaning “he cleaned quickly and melodically”, синилё and мелодиё, fast/quickly and melodical(ly) respectively, do not synthesize their meanings, he is not cleaning to a fast melody.

In order to sythesize, to add the meanings of an adverb and another adverb, or an adverb and a noun or participle, etc. A so-called “Mobile Adverbial Phrase”, which is formed with two particles, у and да, which are glossed as ADVP or MADVP (“P” as in particle) (in a syntax tree diagram it would be “MADVPP”). The MADVP is bound to the main verb phrase, including its auxilliary verbs; can be technically placed anywhere in a sentence, but speakers* tend towards before the verb phrase, in the verb phrase (splitting it in half, sort of) and right after the verb phrase or object.

In the above example, “КИМ ПИРИСТИЛЁ У УЛ ТИХЁ ДА ЛИ ПЫ”, meaning “Something wicked this way comes”—the MADVP in this sentence modifies the verb пи (which is in the middle voice, so it’s пы) to encode it is happening or being performed in “ул тихё”, this direction/way. Because its placement is fluid, it could retain similarity to the original English (анклиш); thus, it was purposefully placed between “ким пиристилё” (something wicked) and “ли пы” (comes).

Participles and adjectives must be inflected like adjectives to become adverbs. Generally participles aren’t converted to adverbs on their own, usually they must be in MADVPs, but then they don’t need to be inflected to be adverbs. Participles, are actually, very rarely turned into adverbs, as their root verbs can be auxilliary verbs instead; for example, “...моты йияш номатамдоликомирҳойё”, meaning “moving while counting/calculating”, is how most if not all participles will be used as adverbs outside of MADVPs.

СОРАТ КАСАМОНАМЁ ДУ ПУЛЙЛОЦКАЦЫ                    The
                                                                                Middle Voice

In СЫПЕҖ, verbs are considered to have three “forms” (сорат ѳэтё), these forms, the “пулйлоцкисората”, or “соратцы ду пулйлоцкацы” are considered seperate from the past tense, which is not considered a form. The three forms are as follows:

  1. The сорат анйимкҳой (*ду пулйлоцкацы), or active voice. This is the null morpheme form of verbs, that is to say, verbs are naturally active.
  2. The сорат демизопжиҳой (*), or passive voice, which, in it’s name, has a passive form participles in it.
  3. The сорат касамонамё (*), middle voice, or, more literally, the empty/missing voice.

        These three voices are simple and they allow for a good amount of variety in what speakers can say and mean. The three voices are grouped into two groups, сората кдепэршонамё, full argument form and сорат касамонамё, missing form. This is determined by a single check: is there both an agent and patient[1] present in the sentence? If no, then the main verb is middle voice, as there is a missing argument. If yes, then the main verb is either active or passive, that depends if the agent is acting on the patient or the patient is acting on the agent.

        Verbs in the middle voice get the suffix, or ending, -ы, which can combine with the past tense suffix -ызат (but it will be identical to -ызат); participles cannot be in the middle voice, but can be passive and active.

        More information on the middle voice particle, партикол походамё омпидэц сорат касамонамё, “ли”, see section “ПАРТИКОЛ ПОХОДАМЁ: ЛИ” (Suspeakan), for more on is other uses.

[1] “agent” and “patient” are deliberate terms instead of “subject” and “object”, as in a sentence like: илинусти ли крсацихы мир, “The sun shines on Earth”, the sun has no agency, it isn’t alive to consciously be shining on purpose, it is a natural process; thus in the middle voice. And also, in situations such as this, adpositions are often ommitted, that is why it isn’t “тимси мир” (on Earth), this is also specific to #the word мир, where it’s adpositions are regularly ommitted.
