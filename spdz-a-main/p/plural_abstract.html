РИѲНОМАТИНОМИНАМ
Number and Pluralization

"Риѳноматиноминам" (Rithnomatinominam) is the term for grammatical number and pluralization in СЫПЕҖ. Nouns in the language are the only parts of speech which are marked for number. Nouns are marked for the plural if there is more than 1 of them, "йейны ай" (one woman), "йейныцы кде" (two women).

The plural suffix is -цы (cí); most of the time it is simply suffixed to the plural noun, but exceptions exist; the two most common of these exceptions are the following

  1. Words like "йлоцк" (meaning "word"), which has a cluster of three consonants at the end. Pluralizing йлоцк would look like: "йлоцкацы", a extra syllable is created as a buffer between the root and the plural suffix. The vowel in the buffer syllable is usually и, or а. Another example, "крсац" (light) becomes "крсацицы".

  2. Inherent or ambiguous, as part of abstraction, see below.


ѲЫМЁ ѲЫАПСТРАЦКТМОҲОЙ-КО
Abstraction

"Ѳымё ѳыапстрацкмоҳой-ко", lit. process of transforming-abstract, is a process/phenomenon of СЫПЕҖ nouns where they become abstract by taking an -а¹ or -ам ending.

Abstraction is a very common phenomenon, and some words may naturally be abstract. Examples of words formed via abstraction include:

  • мота (movement, change), from мот (to move, to change)
  • яйпам (love), from яйпе (to love)
  • йкалам (thankfullness)², from йкал (to thank)
  • мащам (conflict), from мащ (to fight)
  • поҗам (sight), from поҗ (to see/view)

Some examples of words which end in -а¹ but are not neccesarily "abstract" include:

  • миз́а (wind)
  • торя (pain/suffering)
  • нидела (sunday)
  • улиҗа³ (street)
  • илинуста (day)
  • хулам (hill)
  • моста (roof)
  • некута⁴ (sex, as in intercourse)

The other derivational use of -а comes in the form of collectivization, which, in short, makes the noun collective, not plural per se. "...referring to a collection of things taken as a whole"; here are some examples:

  • цодех (human) → цодеха⁵
    not a human, or a certain number of humans, but a group, sort of... like "people" (person plural) or "a people"
  • зинац (food item) → зинаца (food, in general)
  • кил (article, as in of clothing, cloth) → кила (clothing, in general)
  • кхуҗо (house, apartment) → кхуҗа/кхуҗам (housing, in general)
