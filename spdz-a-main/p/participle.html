Participles are an important part of speech to keep in mind. They can only be derived from verbs; their derivation is done by suffixing -ҳой to the approapriat form of the verb. For example:

  • спати (sleep) → спатихой (sleeping), спатызатхой (sleep'ed, rested)
  • мот (move, change) → мотаҳой¹ (moving, changing), мотызатҳой¹ (moved, changed, altered)



[1] мот is seldom used for literal physical movement outside of its present form. Speakers will still say "...йо̄ мот ти..." ("I'm going there"), but in the past tense and as a participle it is used in the developmental and change sense. "тамой ли мотызат зонэцну тамой ли тез́ай дилё" (he has changed )
***(May be an incorect sentence (regarding the adverbial phrase as object), but the use of мот is proper)
